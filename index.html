Sophia Holloway writes Regency romances in the classic tradition, as exemplified by Georgette Heyer. She creates a world of eligible, and sometimes very ineligible, gentlemen, and ladies who vary from those anticipating marriage, those who think the chance has passed them by, and even those who have experienced it once and are resolved to never marry again. 


Her stories are played out against a backdrop that varies from Almack's and Bond Street to affluent country houses, and with casts of characters from the sagacious to the silly. They prove that although the path of true love may not run smooth, the destination is worth the journey.
